Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel, also known as the Iron Man of India, was a pivotal figure in the Indian freedom struggle and played a significant role in the country's integration post-independence. He was the first Deputy Prime Minister and Home Minister of India and is renowned for his leadership in unifying the 565 princely states into the Indian Union.

Born on October 31, 1875, in Gujarat, Patel was a successful lawyer before joining the Indian National Congress and the freedom movement led by Mahatma Gandhi. His organizational skills and dedication to the cause earned him immense respect and trust among his peers and the masses.

Patel's legacy continues to inspire generations. The Statue of Unity, the world's tallest statue, stands as a tribute to his contributions and vision for a united India.
